Diuretics

Hydrochlorothiazide (HCTZ) · Chlorthalidone ★ · Metolazone Thiazide / Thiazide-like Diuretic PO · Once daily · Monitor BMP
⚙ MOA
Block NCC (Na-Cl cotransporter) in DCT → ↓ NaCl reabsorption → ↓ ECF volume → ↓ CO → ↓ BP.

Also ↑ Ca²⁺ reabsorption (basolateral Ca/Na exchanger) → ↓ urinary Ca²⁺ → useful in hypercalciuria/osteoporosis.

Metolazone: retains efficacy even in CKD (GFR<30).
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
Hypokalemia (most common K⁺ wasting)
Hyponatremia
Hyperglycemia (caution in DM)
Hyperuricemia → Gout (contraindicated in gout)
Hypercalcemia (actually useful in osteoporosis)
Hyperlipidemia (↑ LDL, TG)

Contraindicated: Gout, Sulfa allergy, Anuria
🎯 Clinical Uses
1st-line uncomplicated HTNElderly / isolated systolicOsteoporosisHypercalciuriaHF add-on

ALLHAT trial: Chlorthalidone = first-line for most uncomplicated HTN.
Chlorthalidone > HCTZ: longer t½ (50–60 hrs), better CV outcomes.
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
ALLHAT trial: chlorthalidone = first-line uncomplicated HTN (NOT β-blockers, NOT ACEi alone).
Chlorthalidone > HCTZ: longer t½, more potent, better CV outcomes. Prefer chlorthalidone.
ADRs mnemonic — 5 H's + 1 L: Hypo-K, Hypo-Na, Hyper-glucose, Hyper-uricemia, Hyper-calcemia, Hyper-lipidemia.
Hypercalcemia is useful here — good for osteoporosis/hypercalciuria patients.
Metolazone: very potent; add to loop diuretic for diuretic resistance in HF.
Thiazides CONTRAINDICATED in gout (raise uric acid). HTN + gout → use ACEi/ARB/losartan instead. HTN + osteoporosis → thiazide is ideal.
Furosemide (Lasix) · Torsemide Loop Diuretic — NKCC2 Blocker IV (ADHF) or PO · Monitor BMP (K⁺, Mg²⁺)
⚙ MOA
Block NKCC2 in thick ascending loop of Henle → destroys medullary concentration gradient → most potent diuresis available + ↑ Na⁺, K⁺, Mg²⁺ excretion.

Works even in severe renal impairment. Torsemide: longer t½, better oral bioavailability (ideal in RV failure with gut edema).
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
Hypokalemia (significant — wasting)
Hypomagnesemia
Metabolic alkalosis
Ototoxicity (high-dose IV — especially + aminoglycosides)
Hyperuricemia → gout

Contraindicated: Anuria, Sulfa allergy
Note: Loop diuretics ↑ Ca²⁺ excretion (opposite of thiazides)
🎯 Clinical Uses
ADHF — first-lineVolume overloadHypercalcemiaHTN if severe/resistant

NOT first-line for uncomplicated HTN — use thiazides instead.
Torsemide preferred in RV failure (better gut absorption).
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
Loop diuretics ↑ Ca²⁺ excretion — opposite of thiazides (loops lose calcium; thiazides retain it).
IV furosemide = first step in ADHF management.
Ototoxicity with high-dose IV — never combine with aminoglycosides.
Loop diuretics → hypokalemia → ↑ digoxin toxicity. Always monitor K⁺ if patient is on digoxin.
Torsemide: longer t½, better oral bioavailability → preferred in RV failure with gut edema.
Loop diuretics are NOT first-line for uncomplicated HTN. Hypokalemia from loops is the most common precipitant of digoxin toxicity.
Spironolactone · Eplerenone (MRAs) · Triamterene (ENaC blocker) K⁺-Sparing Diuretics PO · Monitor K⁺, Cr · Avoid K⁺ >5.0 or GFR <30
⚙ MOA
MRAs (spiro/eplerenone): Block aldosterone receptor → ↓ Na⁺ retention + ↓ K⁺ excretion + ↓ cardiac fibrosis.

Triamterene: Blocks ENaC channel in collecting duct → ↓ Na⁺ reabsorption + ↓ K⁺ secretion.

Eplerenone = selective MRA (no androgen/progesterone effects).
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
Hyperkalemia (most dangerous — monitor closely)
Metabolic acidosis
Spironolactone only: gynecomastia, impotence, menstrual irregularities (non-selective)

Contraindicated: K⁺ >5.0, GFR <30, combined with ACEi+ARB without monitoring
🎯 Clinical Uses
HTN (add-on to thiazide)Resistant HTN (4th line)HFrEFPrimary hyperaldosteronismHepatic ascites

RALES: spironolactone ↓ mortality in severe HFrEF.
EPHESUS: eplerenone ↓ mortality post-MI HF.
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
Hyperkalemia is the major risk — monitor K⁺ especially when combined with ACEi/ARB.
Gynecomastia/impotence on spironolactone → switch to eplerenone (same efficacy, selective MRA).
RALES trial: spironolactone ↓ HFrEF mortality. EPHESUS: eplerenone post-MI HF.
Add to thiazide to counteract thiazide-induced hypokalemia.
ACEi/ARB + K⁺-sparing = high hyperkalemia risk. Triple RAAS blockade (ACEi + ARB + MRA) → severe hyperkalemia + AKI. NEVER start if K⁺ >5.0.
Empagliflozin · Dapagliflozin SGLT-2 Inhibitors PO · Once daily · Avoid eGFR <20 · Monitor renal function
⚙ MOA
Block SGLT-2 in PCT → ↑ glucose + Na⁺ excretion (osmotic diuresis) → ↓ preload → ↓ BP.

Also direct cardiac/renal anti-remodeling effects independent of glucose. Works in both diabetic and non-diabetic HF.
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
Genital mycotic infections (glucose in urine → yeast)
UTI
Euglycemic DKA (rare — glucose may be NORMAL; check ketones)
Hypotension
Fournier's gangrene (rare)

Contraindicated: eGFR <20, Type 1 DM (↑ DKA risk)
🎯 Clinical Uses
DM type 2 + HTNHFrEF AND HFpEFCKD with proteinuria

Only drug class with mortality benefit in BOTH HFrEF and HFpEF.
EMPA-REG, DAPA-HF, EMPEROR trials.
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
Only class with mortality benefit in BOTH HFrEF AND HFpEF — unique among all CV drugs.
Works in HF even without diabetes — mechanism is beyond glucose lowering.
Euglycemic DKA: DKA with near-normal glucose — always check ketones, not just glucose.
Stop 3–4 days before major surgery (euglycemic DKA risk).
'Patient has no diabetes, so SGLT-2i doesn't apply' → WRONG. SGLT-2i benefit in HF/CKD regardless of DM status.

RAAS Inhibitors

Captopril · Lisinopril · Enalapril (prodrug) ACE Inhibitors — ACEi PO · Monitor K⁺, Cr, BP · Adjust in CKD
⚙ MOA
Block ACE → ↓ Ang II → ↓ vasoconstriction + ↓ aldosterone + ↓ SNS.
Also ↑ bradykinin (ACE normally degrades it) → vasodilation + cough + angioedema risk.

Renoprotective: ↓ efferent arteriole constriction → ↓ intraglomerular pressure → ↓ proteinuria.
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
Dry cough (bradykinin — 10–15%; class effect)
Angioedema (bradykinin — rare but life-threatening)
Hyperkalemia (↓ aldosterone)
AKI (especially bilateral RAS)
Hypotension (1st dose)
Teratogen (Category D/X)

Contraindicated: Bilateral RAS, Pregnancy, K⁺ >5.5, Angioedema history
🎯 Clinical Uses
HTN + DM + Proteinuria ★HFrEFPost-MICKD + Proteinuria

Cr rise <30% after starting = acceptable (therapeutic — ↓ efferent constriction).
Upgrade to ARNI if HFrEF and tolerates.
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
Dry cough = bradykinin class effect → switch to ARB (NOT another ACEi — same class effect).
Angioedema = bradykinin-mediated → switch to ARB; NOT another ACEi or ARNI acutely.
Compelling indications: DM + proteinuria, HFrEF, post-MI, CKD + proteinuria.
Cr rise <30% after starting = acceptable and therapeutic (reduced efferent constriction).
Bilateral RAS: ACEi/ARB CONTRAINDICATED → blocks efferent dilation → severe AKI.
Angioedema from ACEi → do NOT give another ACEi (same bradykinin mechanism). Switch to ARB. Never combine ACEi + ARB — no benefit, ↑ hyperkalemia/AKI.
Losartan · Valsartan · Candesartan Angiotensin Receptor Blockers — ARBs PO · Monitor K⁺, Cr, BP · Adjust in CKD
⚙ MOA
Block AT1 receptor → ↓ Ang II effects → ↓ vasoconstriction + ↓ aldosterone + ↓ remodeling.

NO bradykinin effect → NO cough, very rare angioedema.
Works downstream of chymase pathway → potentially more complete RAAS blockade.
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
Hyperkalemia (same as ACEi)
AKI (bilateral RAS)
Hypotension
NO cough (distinguishing feature vs. ACEi)
Very rare angioedema
Teratogen (Category D/X)

Contraindicated: Bilateral RAS, Pregnancy, severe hyperkalemia
🎯 Clinical Uses
HTN intolerant of ACEi coughDM + ProteinuriaHFrEFPost-MICKD

Losartan is also uricosuric (↓ uric acid) → useful in HTN + gout.
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
ARBs = ACEi minus the cough and angioedema (no bradykinin effect). Same indications.
Use ARB if ACEi causes intolerable cough or angioedema.
Losartan is uricosuric (↓ serum uric acid) — ideal for HTN + gout.
Teratogenic like ACEi — AVOID in pregnancy.
Same compelling indications as ACEi: DM, HF, post-MI, CKD with proteinuria.
Do NOT combine ACEi + ARB (dual RAAS blockade = ↑ hyperkalemia, ↑ AKI, no mortality benefit). Use ONE or the other.
Sacubitril/Valsartan (Entresto) ARB + Neprilysin Inhibitor — ARNI PO · BID · 36-hr ACEi washout! · Monitor K⁺, Cr, BP
⚙ MOA
Sacubitril inhibits neprilysin → ↑ BNP, ANP, bradykinin → ↑ vasodilation + natriuresis + ↓ cardiac fibrosis.
Valsartan blocks AT1R.

PARADIGM-HF: ARNI superior to ACEi → ↓ CV death + HF hospitalization by 20%. Preferred in HFrEF if tolerated.
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
Hypotension (most common — strong vasodilation)
Hyperkalemia
AKI
Angioedema (↑↑ risk if <36 hrs from ACEi — dual bradykinin)

Contraindicated: <36 hrs of ACEi, Pregnancy, Angioedema history, Bilateral RAS
🎯 Clinical Uses
HFrEF (EF ≤40%) — preferred over ACEi

PARADIGM-HF: sacubitril/valsartan ↓ CV death + HF hospitalization 20% vs. enalapril.
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
PARADIGM-HF: ARNI > ACEi in HFrEF — now preferred first-line if tolerated.
36-hr washout from ACEi before starting ARNI — prevent dual bradykinin → angioedema.
Monitor with NT-proBNP (NOT BNP) — neprilysin inhibition falsely elevates BNP.
Hypotension = most common dose-limiting side effect.
ARNI given <36 hrs after ACEi = life-threatening angioedema (both increase bradykinin). NT-proBNP (not BNP) to monitor HF severity on ARNI.

Calcium Channel Blockers (CCBs)

Amlodipine ★ · Nifedipine XR · Nicardipine (IV) Dihydropyridine CCBs — DHP (Vascular-Selective) PO or IV (nicardipine) · No routine monitoring
⚙ MOA
Block L-type Ca²⁺ channels preferentially in vascular smooth muscle → ↓ SVR → ↓ BP.

Minimal cardiac effect — NO AV node effect. May cause reflex tachycardia (short-acting > long-acting).

Nicardipine IV: titratable for HTN emergencies; preferred in neurological settings.
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
Peripheral edema (dependent — vasodilation, NOT fluid retention)
Reflex tachycardia (especially short-acting nifedipine)
Flushing, headache
Gingival hyperplasia (amlodipine/nifedipine)

Avoid: Short-acting nifedipine IR in ACS (reflex tachycardia → ↑ O₂ demand)
Does NOT worsen HFrEF (unlike non-DHP CCBs)
🎯 Clinical Uses
HTN (elderly, isolated systolic)Vasospastic angina ★Raynaud'sHTN emergency (nicardipine IV)HTN in pregnancy (nifedipine XR)

Safe to combine with β-blockers.
Drug of choice for Prinzmetal/vasospastic angina.
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
DHP CCBs: NO AV nodal effect → cannot rate-control Afib (use non-DHP or β-blocker instead).
Peripheral edema = venous dilation, NOT Na/water retention (don't add diuretic for this).
Drug of choice for vasospastic (Prinzmetal) angina and Raynaud's.
Nicardipine IV: titratable, preferred for HTN emergency in neurological settings (SAH).
Amlodipine: long t½ → once daily, slow onset, minimal reflex tachycardia. Preferred oral DHP.
Short-acting nifedipine IR causes reflex tachycardia → dangerous in ACS. Use XR only. DHP CCBs have NO AV nodal effect — useless for Afib rate control.
Verapamil · Diltiazem Non-DHP CCBs (Cardiac + Vascular) PO or IV · Monitor HR, ECG (PR interval) · Check digoxin level
⚙ MOA
Block L-type Ca²⁺ channels in BOTH cardiac and vascular tissue:
• SA node: ↓ automaticity → ↓ HR
• AV node: ↓ conduction → rate control in Afib
• Vascular: ↓ SVR → ↓ BP

Verapamil: most cardiac selectivity. Diltiazem: intermediate.
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
Bradycardia and AV block (especially verapamil)
Constipation (verapamil — most constipating antihypertensive)
Hypotension
↑ Digoxin levels (verapamil inhibits P-gp)
Negative inotropy → worsen HFrEF

Contraindicated: HFrEF, combined with β-blocker (→ heart block), WPW + Afib, Bradycardia
🎯 Clinical Uses
HTN + Afib rate controlSVT (verapamil IV)AnginaHOCM (symptom relief)
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
Non-DHP CCBs have AV nodal effect → used for Afib/AFL rate control AND HTN (dual benefit).
Verapamil inhibits P-gp → ↑ digoxin levels → check digoxin if both prescribed.
Constipation = classic verapamil SE (most constipating antihypertensive — classic boards question).
Non-DHP CCBs WORSEN HFrEF — avoid in systolic dysfunction.
Non-DHP + β-blocker = DANGEROUS → complete AV block/cardiac arrest.
Non-DHP + β-blocker = AV block. Non-DHP in HFrEF = worsens EF. Verapamil + digoxin = digoxin toxicity (P-gp inhibition).

β-Blockers

Metoprolol · Bisoprolol · Carvedilol · Labetalol · Propranolol · Nebivolol · Esmolol (IV) β-Blockers — Various Selectivity PO or IV (labetalol, esmolol) · NEVER stop abruptly
⚙ MOA
β₁ blockade → ↓ HR + ↓ contractility → ↓ CO → ↓ BP. Also ↓ renin release (β₁ in JGA).

Carvedilol/Labetalol: α₁ + β blockade → ↓ SVR additionally.
Nebivolol: β₁ + ↑ NO → vasodilation.
Esmolol: t½ ~9 min (erythrocyte esterase) → IV only, rapidly titratable.
Propranolol: non-selective, crosses BBB (migraine, tremor, anxiety).
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
Bradycardia, AV block, hypotension
Fatigue, sexual dysfunction, depression
Bronchoconstriction (non-selective β₂ block — avoid in asthma/COPD)
Masks hypoglycemia tachycardia (sweating preserved)
Hyperglycemia/hypertriglyceridemia (non-selective)

NEVER stop abruptly → rebound HTN/angina/MI
🎯 Clinical Uses
Post-MI ★ (survival benefit)HFrEF (3 specific drugs)AnginaAfib rate controlHTN emergency in pregnancy (labetalol IV)

NOT first-line for uncomplicated HTN per ALLHAT.
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
NOT first-line for uncomplicated HTN (ALLHAT: inferior to thiazides).
Only 3 β-blockers for HFrEF mortality: carvedilol, metoprolol succinate, bisoprolol.
Labetalol IV: drug of choice for HTN emergency in pregnancy (α+β block, no fetal cyanide risk).
Esmolol: ultrashort t½ (~9 min) — IV only; acute rate control, aortic dissection.
Propranolol: non-selective, crosses BBB → migraine prophylaxis, essential tremor, hyperthyroidism.
NEVER stop abruptly → rebound HTN/angina/MI. Always taper.
Non-selective β-blockers in asthma → bronchoconstriction. Metoprolol TARTRATE ≠ metoprolol SUCCINATE (only succinate for HFrEF mortality). β-blockers mask tachycardia of hypoglycemia (sweating preserved).

α-Blockers & Central Sympatholytics

Prazosin · Doxazosin (α₁) · Tamsulosin (α₁A) · Clonidine · Methyldopa α-Blockers & Central Sympatholytics PO · Prazosin: start low at bedtime · Clonidine: patch or oral
⚙ MOA
α₁-blockers (prazosin, doxazosin): Block α₁ receptors → ↓ arteriolar + venous tone → ↓ SVR → ↓ BP.
Tamsulosin: α₁A-selective → prostate relaxation (less systemic hypotension).

Clonidine: Central α₂ agonist → ↓ NE release → ↓ HR + ↓ SVR.
Methyldopa: prodrug → α-methylNE → central α₂ agonism.
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
First-dose orthostatic hypotension (syncope — prazosin/doxazosin)
Reflex tachycardia
Clonidine: dry mouth, sedation
Clonidine abrupt withdrawal → rebound hypertensive crisis
Methyldopa: positive Coombs test (hemolytic anemia), sedation, hepatotoxicity
ALLHAT: doxazosin arm → ↑ HF risk vs. chlorthalidone
🎯 Clinical Uses
HTN + BPH (doxazosin, prazosin)Refractory HTN (clonidine)HTN in Pregnancy (methyldopa)Opioid withdrawal (clonidine)

Not first-line for uncomplicated HTN.
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
First-dose orthostatic hypotension with prazosin → start low dose at BEDTIME.
α₁-blockers: dual benefit in HTN + BPH. Tamsulosin = prostate-selective only (less hypotension).
Clonidine abrupt withdrawal → rebound hypertensive crisis. ALWAYS taper.
Methyldopa → positive Coombs test (direct Coombs+, hemolytic anemia). Drug of choice for HTN historically in pregnancy.
Labetalol IV now preferred over methyldopa for acute HTN in pregnancy.
Clonidine: NEVER stop abruptly — rebound HTN. Methyldopa → positive Coombs (hemolytic anemia) + sedation. ALLHAT showed doxazosin ↑ HF risk.

Direct Vasodilators & HTN Emergencies

Hydralazine · Minoxidil · Nitroprusside (IV) Direct Vasodilators Hydralazine: PO or IV · Nitroprusside: IV ICU only · Always add β-blocker + diuretic
⚙ MOA
Hydralazine: Direct arteriolar vasodilation (↓ intracellular Ca²⁺) → ↓ SVR → reflex tachycardia + RAAS activation.
Minoxidil: K⁺ channel opener → membrane hyperpolarization → ↓ Ca²⁺ entry → vasodilation.
Nitroprusside: NO donor → ↑ cGMP → balanced arterial + venous vasodilation. Most potent.
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
Hydralazine: reflex tachycardia, Na/water retention
Drug-induced lupus (high dose — ANA+, anti-histone+, anti-dsDNA NEGATIVE)
Minoxidil: hypertrichosis (hair growth — topical → Rogaine), fluid retention, pericardial effusion
Nitroprusside: cyanide toxicity (prolonged use/renal failure) → monitor thiocyanate
Avoid nitroprusside in pregnancy (fetal cyanide toxicity)
🎯 Clinical Uses
Resistant HTN (hydralazine triple therapy)HFrEF (hydralazine + ISDN)HTN emergency in pregnancy (hydralazine IV)Alopecia (minoxidil topical)HTN emergency (nitroprusside IV)

Hydralazine always needs β-blocker + diuretic.
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
Hydralazine ALWAYS needs β-blocker + diuretic — reflex tachycardia + fluid retention blunt effect alone.
Drug-induced lupus from hydralazine: ANA+, anti-histone+, anti-dsDNA NEGATIVE (vs. idiopathic SLE: anti-dsDNA+).
Minoxidil → hypertrichosis (body hair growth) → topical version = Rogaine for alopecia.
Nitroprusside → cyanide toxicity in prolonged use or renal failure. Monitor thiocyanate. NEVER in pregnancy.
IV labetalol > nitroprusside for pregnancy HTN emergency (no cyanide risk).
Hydralazine-induced lupus: ANA (+), anti-histone (+), anti-dsDNA NEGATIVE (not like idiopathic lupus). Nitroprusside in pregnancy → fetal cyanide toxicity. Never use nitroprusside alone without ICU monitoring.
