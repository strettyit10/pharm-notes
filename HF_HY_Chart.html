Spironolactone · Eplerenone MRAs — Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonists PO · Monitor K⁺ closely · Avoid K⁺ >5.0 or GFR <30
⚙ MOA
Block mineralocorticoid (aldosterone) receptor → ↓ Na⁺ retention + ↓ K⁺ excretion + ↓ cardiac fibrosis/remodeling.

In HF: high aldosterone drives myocardial fibrosis → MRA blocks → ↓ remodeling.
Eplerenone = selective MRA (no androgen/progesterone effects).

Hyperkalemia (most serious — monitor with ACEi/ARB)

β-Blockers — Only 3 Specific Drugs Proven in HFrEF

Carvedilol ★ · Metoprolol Succinate ★ · Bisoprolol ★ β-Blockers — Specific HFrEF-Approved Drugs Only PO · Start LOW, titrate SLOWLY · NEVER start in ADHF
⚙ MOA
Block chronic SNS catecholamine excess in HF → ↓ catecholamine toxicity → ↓ remodeling → ↑ EF over weeks-months.

PARADOX: Negative inotropes that IMPROVE long-term cardiac function via reverse remodeling.

Carvedilol: α₁+β₁+β₂ (most vasodilation). Metoprolol succinate, bisoprolol: β₁-selective.
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
Bradycardia, hypotension (especially carvedilol — α₁ block)
Fatigue, dizziness
Fluid retention (if titrated too fast)
Worsening HF if started during acute decompensation

Contraindicated: ADHF/cardiogenic shock, Severe bronchospasm, Bradycardia/AV block
Start only when euvolemic and hemodynamically stable
🎯 Clinical Uses
HFrEF (EF ≤40%) — mortality benefit

Only these 3 drugs proven: carvedilol, metoprolol SUCCINATE, bisoprolol.
Other β-blockers (atenolol, propranolol) NOT proven in HFrEF.
EF improvement takes weeks to months.
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
Only 3 β-blockers proven for HFrEF mortality: carvedilol, metoprolol succinate, bisoprolol.
Paradox: negative inotrope that improves EF long-term via reverse remodeling.
NEVER start in ADHF → can precipitate cardiogenic shock.
Start ONLY when euvolemic and hemodynamically stable.
EF improvement takes weeks to months — inform patients.
Metoprolol TARTRATE (immediate-release) ≠ metoprolol SUCCINATE. Only succinate is proven for HFrEF mortality. Do NOT substitute. Never start β-blocker in acute decompensated HF.

⚙ MOA
Block SGLT-2 in PCT → osmotic diuresis + natriuresis + direct cardiac anti-fibrotic/anti-inflammatory effects.

Mechanism in HF goes BEYOND glucose lowering — works in both diabetic and non-diabetic HF patients.

Euglycemic DKA: DKA with near-normal glucose — check ketones, not just glucose.
EMPEROR-Preserved/Reduced (empagliflozin); DAPA-HF (dapagliflozin): landmark trials.
'No diabetes → SGLT-2i doesn't apply' → WRONG. SGLT-2i benefit in HF regardless of DM. Euglycemic DKA — glucose may be NORMAL.
Ivabradine (Corlanor) If (Funny Current) Inhibitor — Pure HR Reducer PO · BID · HR ≥70 bpm required · Sinus rhythm ONLY
⚙ MOA
Blocks If (funny current) in SA node → ↓ pacemaker depolarization rate → ↓ HR ONLY.

No effect on contractility, BP, or conduction below SA node.
NO effect in Atrial Fibrillation (SA node not driving rhythm in Afib).
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
Bradycardia
Luminous phenomena (phosphenes) — visual brightness; reversible and characteristic
Headache

Contraindicated: Afib/flutter, HR <60, Sick sinus syndrome, Strong CYP3A4 inhibitors
🎯 Clinical Uses
HFrEF + HR ≥70 in sinus rhythm on max β-blocker

SHIFT trial: ivabradine ↓ HF hospitalizations.
Add-on when HR goal not achieved with max β-blocker.
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
Indication: HFrEF + sinus rhythm + HR ≥70 on maximum tolerated β-blocker dose.
Pure rate-reducer — no effect on contractility, BP, or AV conduction.
COMPLETELY USELESS IN AFIB — only blocks SA node (not driving rhythm in Afib).
Luminous phenomena (phosphenes) = unique and characteristic ADR of ivabradine.
Ivabradine for Afib rate control → WRONG. Only works in sinus rhythm. Also useless if HR <60 or patient is already on max β-blocker and HR is adequate.

Symptom Relief — Diuretics & Vasodilators (No Mortality Benefit)

Furosemide (IV for ADHF) · Torsemide (oral preferred) Loop Diuretics — NKCC2 Block — Symptom Relief Only IV (ADHF) or PO · Monitor BMP (K⁺, Mg²⁺, Cr)
⚙ MOA
Block NKCC2 in thick ascending loop of Henle → intense natriuresis + diuresis → ↓ ECF volume → ↓ preload → ↓ pulmonary congestion.

Rapidly relieve dyspnea and volume overload. Do NOT improve mortality in HFrEF.
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
Hypokalemia (critical — ↑ digoxin toxicity!)
Hypomagnesemia
Metabolic alkalosis
Ototoxicity (high-dose IV, especially + aminoglycosides)
Hyperuricemia
Dehydration/AKI from overdiuresis
🎯 Clinical Uses
ADHF — IV furosemide FIRST-LINEChronic HFrEF volume management

Diuretic resistance → add metolazone (synergistic DCT blockade).
Torsemide: better oral bioavailability → preferred in RV failure.
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
Diuretics relieve SYMPTOMS but do NOT reduce mortality in HFrEF.
IV furosemide = first step in ADHF management.
Hypokalemia from loops → ↑ digoxin toxicity (K⁺ competes at Na/K-ATPase).
Diuretic resistance → add metolazone (synergistic blockade of DCT + loop).
Torsemide: better oral bioavailability than furosemide → preferred in RV failure with gut edema.
Loop diuretic + digoxin → always monitor K⁺. Furosemide causes ototoxicity at high IV doses (especially with aminoglycosides). Diuretics = symptom relief only, NOT mortality benefit.
Hydralazine + Isosorbide Dinitrate (H-ISDN / BiDil) Arteriolar + Venodilator Combo — HFrEF Alternative PO · TID · Nitrate-free interval required (8–10 hrs)
⚙ MOA
Hydralazine: direct arteriolar vasodilation → ↓ SVR (↓ afterload).
Isosorbide dinitrate: ↑ NO → ↑ cGMP → venodilation → ↓ preload.

Complementary: together ↓ both preload AND afterload.
A-HeFT trial: specifically benefited Black patients with HFrEF.
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
Hydralazine: reflex tachycardia, headache
Drug-induced lupus (high dose — ANA+, anti-histone+, anti-dsDNA NEGATIVE)
Nitrate tolerance (8–10 hr nitrate-free period required daily)
Hypotension

Contraindicated: PDE-5 inhibitors + nitrates = severe hypotension
🎯 Clinical Uses
HFrEF when ACEi/ARB/ARNI NOT tolerated★ Black patients with HFrEF on standard therapy (A-HeFT)

A-HeFT: H-ISDN ↓ mortality 43% in self-identified Black HFrEF patients on standard therapy.
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
Two specific indications: (1) ACEi/ARB/ARNI intolerant HFrEF and (2) Black patients with HFrEF on standard therapy (A-HeFT).
A-HeFT trial: H-ISDN added to standard therapy in Black HFrEF → ↓ mortality 43%.
Drug-induced lupus: ANA+, anti-histone+, anti-dsDNA NEGATIVE.
Nitrate tolerance → must have 8–10 hr nitrate-free interval daily.
H-ISDN + PDE-5 inhibitor (sildenafil, tadalafil) = severe hypotension. CONTRAINDICATED. Hydralazine lupus: ANA+, anti-histone+, anti-dsDNA NEGATIVE (not idiopathic SLE).

Digoxin & Other Agents

Digoxin (Digitalis) Cardiac Glycoside — Na/K-ATPase Inhibitor — NO Mortality Benefit PO or IV · Narrow TI · Target 0.5–0.9 ng/mL · Monitor K⁺, Mg²⁺, Cr
⚙ MOA
Inhibits Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase → ↑ intracellular Na⁺ → reverse NCX → ↑ intracellular Ca²⁺ → positive inotropy.

Also vagomimetic → ↑ vagal tone → ↓ HR + ↓ AV conduction → rate control in Afib.

Narrow therapeutic index: target 0.5–0.9 ng/mL for HF (NOT the old 1.0–2.0 range).
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
Toxicity triad:
GI: N/V, anorexia (earliest signs)
CNS: Xanthopsia (yellow-green visual halos), confusion
Cardiac: any arrhythmia (PAT with block is classic), AV block, bidirectional VT

Toxicity precipitated by:
• HYPOKALEMIA (most important — K⁺ competes at binding site)
• Renal failure, hypomagnesemia, hypercalcemia
• Verapamil/amiodarone → ↑ digoxin levels (P-gp inhibition)

Treatment: DIGIBIND (anti-digoxin Fab antibodies)
🎯 Clinical Uses
HFrEF symptom relief (no mortality benefit)Afib rate control

DIG trial: digoxin ↓ HF hospitalizations but NO mortality benefit.
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
NO MORTALITY BENEFIT — reduces symptoms and hospitalizations only (DIG trial).
Therapeutic level for HF: 0.5–0.9 ng/mL (NOT old 1.0–2.0 range).
Hypokalemia dramatically ↑ digoxin toxicity — K⁺ competes at Na/K-ATPase binding site.
Toxicity triad: GI (N/V) + yellow-green halos (xanthopsia) + cardiac arrhythmia (PAT with block).
Toxicity treatment: DIGIBIND (Fab antibody fragments).
Verapamil + amiodarone → ↑ digoxin levels (P-gp inhibition) → toxicity.
Loop diuretic + digoxin → always monitor K⁺ (hypokalemia = most common toxicity precipitant). Target level 0.5–0.9 ng/mL for HF (NOT 1.0–2.0). PAT with block = classic digoxin toxicity arrhythmia.
