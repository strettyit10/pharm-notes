Statins — HMG-CoA Reductase Inhibitors (Primary LDL-Lowering)

Atorvastatin ★ · Rosuvastatin ★ · Pravastatin · Simvastatin HMG-CoA Reductase Inhibitors — Statins PO · Once daily (evening preferred) · Monitor CK if myalgia · Baseline LFTs
⚙ MOA
Inhibit HMG-CoA reductase (rate-limiting step of cholesterol synthesis) → ↓ hepatic cholesterol → compensatory ↑ LDL receptor expression → ↑ LDL clearance → ↓ serum LDL.

High-intensity: ↓ LDL ≥50%. Moderate: ↓ LDL 30–50%.

All metabolized by CYP3A4 EXCEPT pravastatin (glucuronidation → fewer drug interactions).
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
Myopathy/myalgia (most common) → check CK
Rhabdomyolysis (↑↑ CK + myoglobinuria + AKI) → STOP immediately, hydrate aggressively
↑ LFTs (monitor at baseline; routine monitoring not required if asymptomatic)
Teratogenic (Category X) — ABSOLUTELY CONTRAINDICATED in pregnancy
↑ Glucose / new-onset DM (slight risk)

Myopathy risk ↑ with: high dose, CYP3A4 inhibitors (clarithromycin, azoles, grapefruit juice), gemfibrozil co-administration
🎯 Clinical Uses
Primary ASCVD preventionSecondary prevention (post-MI, stroke, PAD)Familial hypercholesterolemiaAny elevated LDL-C

First-line drug for LDL lowering.
Step-up: Statin → + ezetimibe → + PCSK9 inhibitor.
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
High-intensity: atorvastatin, rosuvastatin (LDL ↓ ≥50%). Know these two.
Pravastatin: glucuronidation (NOT CYP3A4) → fewer drug interactions → safer to combine with fibrates if needed.
Simvastatin: multiple CYP3A4 interactions; max 20 mg/day with amlodipine (↑ myopathy risk).
Rhabdomyolysis: myalgia + ↑ CK + myoglobinuria + AKI → STOP statin immediately.
Teratogenic (Category X) — NEVER in pregnancy.
SIMVASTATIN + GEMFIBROZIL = classic rhabdomyolysis combo (gemfibrozil inhibits glucuronidation of simvastatin → ↑ statin plasma levels). If fibrate + statin needed: use FENOFIBRATE + PRAVASTATIN (safer combination).

Fibrates — Fibric Acids (Primary TG-Lowering)

Fenofibrate (Tricor) ★ safer with statins · Gemfibrozil (Lopid) ★ AVOID with statins Fibrates — PPARα Agonists PO · With meals · Monitor CK, LFTs · Adjust in CKD
⚙ MOA
Activate PPARα (peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor alpha) → ↑ lipoprotein lipase (LPL) + ↑ ApoA-I + ↓ ApoC-III → ↑ VLDL hydrolysis → ↓ TG 40–60% + ↑ HDL 10–20%.

Gemfibrozil: inhibits statin glucuronidation → ↑ statin plasma levels → DANGEROUS with statins.
Fenofibrate: does NOT inhibit glucuronidation → SAFER with statins.
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
Myopathy (risk ↑ with statins, especially gemfibrozil)
Cholelithiasis (gallstones) — ↑ biliary cholesterol saturation → classic association
↑ LFTs, GI upset
Gemfibrozil + ANY statin = rhabdomyolysis risk

Contraindicated: Gemfibrozil + statins (rhabdomyolysis), severe hepatic/renal disease, gallbladder disease
🎯 Clinical Uses
★ Severe hypertriglyceridemia (TG >500 mg/dL) — pancreatitis preventionMixed dyslipidemia (↑ TG + ↓ HDL)

Fenofibrate preferred if fibrate + statin needed.
NOT for isolated LDL elevation (statins are better).
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
First-line for TG >500 mg/dL (pancreatitis prevention). Primary use = hypertriglyceridemia.
GEMFIBROZIL: inhibits statin glucuronidation → ↑ statin plasma levels → rhabdomyolysis.
FENOFIBRATE: safer with statins (does NOT inhibit glucuronidation). Always prefer if fibrate + statin needed.
Cholelithiasis: fibrates ↑ biliary cholesterol concentration → gallstones (classic association).
Gemfibrozil + any statin = most dangerous drug interaction in lipid pharmacology. ALWAYS use fenofibrate if fibrate + statin combination is needed. Fibrates can slightly ↑ LDL-C in some patients.

Bile Acid Sequestrants (BAS) — Anion Exchange Resins

Cholestyramine (Questran) · Colesevelam (WelChol) ★ Bile Acid Sequestrants — Anion Exchange Resins PO · With meals · NOT systemically absorbed · Other meds: 1 hr before or 4 hrs after
⚙ MOA
Anion exchange resins bind bile acids in intestinal lumen → ↓ BA enterohepatic circulation → hepatocytes convert more cholesterol to BAs → ↑ LDL receptor expression → ↓ serum LDL 15–30%.

NOT systemically absorbed → safe in pregnancy.
Colesevelam: also approved for type 2 DM (modestly ↓ glucose).
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
Constipation, bloating, nausea (most common — GI effects)
Drug absorption impaired: fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K), warfarin, digoxin, levothyroxine, tetracyclines
↑ Triglycerides (contraindicated if TG elevated — raises TG further)

Contraindicated: Hypertriglyceridemia, Complete biliary obstruction
Take all other meds 1 hr BEFORE or 4 hrs AFTER BAS
🎯 Clinical Uses
★ Pregnancy + hypercholesterolemia (safest option)Pediatric FHPrimary hypercholesterolemia (add-on to statin)Colesevelam: DM type 2 (modest glucose ↓)
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
Safe in PREGNANCY — not systemically absorbed (no fetal exposure). Drug of choice for hypercholesterolemia in pregnancy.
Colesevelam: tablets (easier than powder), also approved for type 2 DM (modest glucose ↓).
Drug interactions: impair absorption of fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K) + warfarin, digoxin, levothyroxine.
AVOID if TG elevated — BAS raise TG.
Constipation = most common complaint.
BAS + warfarin → ↓ warfarin absorption → ↓ INR. BAS RAISE TG — contraindicated in hypertriglyceridemia. Pregnancy + hypercholesterolemia → bile acid sequestrant (NOT statin — teratogenic).

Cholesterol Absorption Inhibitor — Add-On Therapy

Ezetimibe (Zetia) NPC1L1 Blocker — Intestinal Cholesterol Absorption Inhibitor PO · Once daily · No routine monitoring · Safe to combine with statins
⚙ MOA
Blocks NPC1L1 (Niemann-Pick C1-Like 1) transporter in intestinal brush border → ↓ intestinal cholesterol absorption → ↓ LDL ~18–25%.

When combined with statin: synergistic effect (statin ↓ synthesis; ezetimibe ↓ absorption) → additional 20–25% LDL reduction.
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
Generally very well tolerated
Rare: ↑ LFTs, myopathy (additive with statin — uncommon)
Rare: GI upset

Contraindications: none absolute
🎯 Clinical Uses
Add-on when statin alone doesn't reach LDL goalStatin intoleranceFamilial hypercholesterolemia

IMPROVED-IT: ezetimibe + statin ↓ CV events vs. statin alone.
Step-up: Statin → + ezetimibe → + PCSK9 inhibitor.
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
Modest LDL reduction (~18–25%) alone; synergistic with statin (additional ~20% reduction).
Step-up logic: max statin → + ezetimibe → + PCSK9 inhibitor.
IMPROVED-IT trial: ezetimibe + statin ↓ CV events (modest benefit).
Very well tolerated — no major safety concerns.
Useful in statin-intolerant patients: ezetimibe ± PCSK9 inhibitor.
Ezetimibe ALONE has only MODEST effect — not comparable to statins for ASCVD prevention. Always start statin first (unless statin intolerant). Ezetimibe = add-on, not first-line monotherapy.

PCSK9 Inhibitors — Most Potent LDL-Lowering (Add-On Only)

Evolocumab (Repatha) · Alirocumab (Praluent) PCSK9 Inhibitors — Anti-PCSK9 Monoclonal Antibodies SQ injection · Every 2 or 4 weeks · No routine monitoring · Expensive
⚙ MOA
Monoclonal antibodies → bind PCSK9 → prevent PCSK9 from degrading LDL receptors → LDL receptors recycled back to hepatocyte surface → ↑ LDL receptor density → ↑ LDL clearance → ↓ LDL 50–60%.

Most potent LDL-lowering agents available. Injectable only (SQ).
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
Injection site reactions (mild)
Nasopharyngitis, flu-like symptoms (mild)
Rare: neurocognitive effects (under investigation; appears rare/reversible)

No absolute contraindications. Cost/insurance barriers are common.
Use ONLY after exhausting statin ± ezetimibe options (unless statin intolerant)
🎯 Clinical Uses
★ Familial hypercholesterolemia (FH) — esp. homozygousVery high ASCVD risk + LDL not at goal on max statin + ezetimibeStatin intolerance

FOURIER (evolocumab): ↓ CV events ~15% vs. placebo on top of statin.
ODYSSEY (alirocumab): ↓ CV events in post-ACS.
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
Most potent LDL-lowering: ↓ LDL 50–60% (on top of statin effect).
NEVER first-line — always exhaust statin ± ezetimibe options first.
Step-up: Statin → + ezetimibe → + PCSK9 inhibitor.
Injectable (SQ) every 2–4 weeks — expensive, requires prior authorization.
FH (especially homozygous FH) — primary indication.
'PCSK9 inhibitors are first-line for high LDL' → WRONG. Always start statin first. PCSK9 inhibitors are add-on therapy after statin ± ezetimibe. Indicated primarily for FH or very high ASCVD risk not meeting goals.

Omega-3 Fatty Acids — TG-Lowering

Icosapentaenoic Acid / EPA (Vascepa ★) · Omega-3-acid ethyl esters (Lovaza) · OTC Fish Oils Omega-3 Fatty Acids — PPARα + VLDL Synthesis Reduction PO · With meals · Prescription formulation needed for therapeutic effect
⚙ MOA
EPA (and DHA) → ↓ hepatic VLDL synthesis + ↓ TG secretion → ↓ TG 30–50% at therapeutic doses (4g/day).

Vascepa (pure EPA): REDUCE-IT trial → ↓ CV events in high-CV-risk patients with elevated TG on statin therapy.

Note: DHA-containing formulations may slightly ↑ LDL-C in some patients.
⚡ Side Effects & Contraindications
GI upset, fishy aftertaste/breath ('fishy burp')
↑ Bleeding time (mild antiplatelet effect — caution with anticoagulants)
DHA formulations: may slightly ↑ LDL-C

OTC fish oils: variable, unreliable potency — NOT equivalent to prescription formulations
🎯 Clinical Uses
★ Severe hypertriglyceridemia (TG >500 mg/dL) — pancreatitis preventionHigh CV risk on statin with elevated TG (Vascepa — REDUCE-IT)

OTC fish oils are dietary supplements only — NOT for therapeutic TG lowering.
⭐ High-Yield Facts (Exam Focus)
REDUCE-IT trial: Vascepa (pure EPA, 4g/day) ↓ CV events 25% in statin-treated patients with TG 135–500.
Prescription formulations required for therapeutic TG lowering — OTC fish oils have variable, unreliable potency.
DHA-containing formulations may slightly ↑ LDL-C — watch in mixed dyslipidemia.
TG >500 mg/dL = risk of pancreatitis → fibrate OR omega-3 FA.
OTC fish oils ≠ therapeutic prescription omega-3 FA (Vascepa/Lovaza). Cannot substitute OTC fish oil capsules for clinical TG management. Vascepa (EPA only) ≠ Lovaza (EPA+DHA) — different CV evidence.

Treatment Step-Up by Lipid Pattern

Isolated ↑ LDL ★ High-intensity statin → + ezetimibe → + PCSK9 inhibitor Statins are FIRST-LINE. Never skip to PCSK9 inhibitors.
TG >500 mg/dL (pancreatitis risk) ★ Fibrate (fenofibrate preferred) OR Omega-3 FA (Vascepa) ⚠ BAS contraindicated (raise TG). Use fenofibrate if combining with statin.
Mixed dyslipidemia (↑ LDL + ↑ TG) ★ Statin (for LDL) + fenofibrate if needed (for TG) ⚠ Use FENOFIBRATE (not gemfibrozil) with statins.
Pregnancy + ↑ LDL ★ Bile acid sequestrant (cholestyramine or colesevelam) ⚠ Statins TERATOGENIC (Category X) — NEVER in pregnancy.
Statin intolerance ★ Ezetimibe ± PCSK9 inhibitor Try lower-dose or alternate statin (rosuvastatin, pravastatin) first.
Familial Hypercholesterolemia (FH) ★ High-intensity statin → + ezetimibe → + PCSK9 inhibitor (evolocumab) PCSK9 inhibitors key in homozygous FH (LDL-R mutation reduces statin effect).
Post-ACS (secondary prevention) ★ HIGH-intensity statin (atorvastatin 80 mg or rosuvastatin 40 mg) After ACS: highest-intensity statin regardless of baseline LDL. Target LDL <70.
DM + ↑ LDL ★ Statin (primary risk reduction) Colesevelam = dual benefit (↓ LDL + modest ↓ glucose). SGLT-2i if HF/CKD also present.
